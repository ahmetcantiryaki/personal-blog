# Article thread — GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

> Source article: /en/posts/generative-engine-optimization-guide
> Link ONLY in [REPLY]. Good bookmark bait — numbered, actionable.

---

**1/**
Google sent my blog 1,000 visitors last month.

ChatGPT sent 40 — but they converted 9x better.

AI search is small and it's already the highest-intent traffic that exists.
Here's how to get LLMs to cite you (GEO, in 6 steps): 🧵

**2/**
1. Freshness wins.

AI engines weigh recency hard. A 2024 guide loses to a 2026 article on the
same topic, almost every time.

Update dates honestly. Re-verify facts. Stale content is invisible content.

**3/**
2. Answer real questions, literally.

LLMs retrieve passages, not pages. A clear H3 question + a direct 2-3 sentence
answer is the exact shape they lift.

(This is why every article on my blog ends with a real FAQ + FAQPage schema.)

**4/**
3. Entities > keywords.

AI engines map who is authoritative on what. Cover a topic cluster deeply
instead of scattering one-off posts. Internal links between related articles
build that map for you.

**5/**
4. Be citable: concrete numbers, named sources, primary links.

LLMs prefer passages that carry verifiable facts. "Conversion improved 42%
(Adobe, 2026)" gets cited. "Conversions improved a lot" doesn't.

**6/**
5. Community presence matters — about half of AI citations come from
community platforms, not blogs.

6. Measure it: check your analytics for chatgpt.com / perplexity.ai referrers
monthly. Tools like Profound and Otterly track AI visibility directly.

**7/**
The uncomfortable part: Gartner expects traditional search volume to keep
falling as answers move into AI engines.

The sites that adapt their content shape now will own the citations later.

Bookmark this for your next content review.
