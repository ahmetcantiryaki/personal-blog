The most popular AI tools in 2026, ranked by real usage, are led by ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Anthropic's Claude. ChatGPT alone reached roughly 1 billion monthly active app users in June 2026. Below you will find the top 10 tools, their free tiers, July 2026 prices, and which one fits which job — ranked by measurable use, not marketing claims.

## How we ranked this: real use, not hype

This list rests on two concrete data points: monthly active user (MAU) counts and [Similarweb's ranking of the most-visited AI websites](https://www.similarweb.com/top-websites/ai-chatbots-and-tools/). Calling a tool "the best" is subjective; counting how many people actually use it is not. So the most honest answer to "what are the most popular AI tools" comes from traffic and user numbers, not ad campaigns.

We weigh the two metrics together. A tool with high MAU spread across a narrower surface, such as Microsoft Copilot, can look lower than it deserves if you only read web traffic — so the ranking is a blend of both. Because different sources measure different things — some count app MAU, some web visits, some valuation — treat this list less as a fixed scoreboard and more as a compass that points in the right direction. All prices are in US dollars and current as of July 2026.

## The most popular AI tools in 2026

**1. ChatGPT — OpenAI.** [Roughly 1 billion monthly active app users in June 2026](https://www.reuters.com/technology/) and 900 million weekly active users; the most-visited AI website on Similarweb. *Best for:* all-round chat, writing, research, and coding.

**2. Google Gemini.** More than 900 million monthly active users announced at Google I/O in May 2026 (up from 400 million a year earlier); reach climbs toward 2 billion once AI Overviews are counted. Second by web traffic. *Best for:* multimodal tasks, Google Workspace integration, and a huge context window.

**3. Claude — Anthropic.** The third most-visited AI website in the world per Similarweb; its web traffic grew roughly 770% between September 2024 and March 2026. *Best for:* long-document analysis, careful writing, and coding.

**4. DeepSeek.** Fourth by traffic, with 350.8 million web visits in March 2026, around 96.9 million MAU globally, and 81.6 million weekly active users in China. *Best for:* low-cost, open-weight reasoning.

**5. Grok — xAI.** The fifth most-visited AI website on Similarweb, with the longest average session of the top tier (about 11 minutes). *Best for:* real-time X and web knowledge, plus unfiltered, playful answers.

**6. Microsoft Copilot.** 420 million monthly active users across every surface (Windows, Edge, Microsoft 365, Bing, and mobile) as of the first quarter of 2026. *Best for:* office productivity inside Word, Excel, and Teams.

**7. Perplexity.** Around 45 million users processing more than 1 billion queries a month; a $22.6 billion valuation and over $450 million in annual revenue (FT, March 2026). *Best for:* AI search that cites its sources.

**8. Character.AI.** The seventh most-visited AI website and, by far, the highest engagement in the top 10 (about a 15:34 average session). *Best for:* character-based chat and roleplay.

**9. Midjourney.** The largest dedicated AI image tool: roughly 20 million registered users, 1.2M–2.5M daily active users, and over $500 million in annual revenue in 2026. *Best for:* artistic, high-end image generation.

**10. Canva (Magic Studio).** The seventh most popular generative-AI consumer web app on a16z's March 2026 list, with AI features embedded across its mass-market design suite. *Best for:* everyday content that blends design and AI in one place.

The shape of the list tells its own story: general-purpose chatbots hold the top three spots, while the remaining seven tools spread across verticals like search, image generation, office work, and character chat. There is no single winner — people increasingly switch between tools by task. So the real question is not "which is best" but "which one for which job."

## Which one should you pick? By use case

The right tool depends on what you use it for. Here are clear picks for five common jobs.

**Chat, writing, and general use.** For most people, ChatGPT Plus ($20) or Claude Pro ($20) covers nearly all daily work. If you are starting with ChatGPT for the first time, our [complete ChatGPT guide](/en/posts/chatgpt-complete-guide-2026) walks through which plan fits you. To pit the two giants against each other directly, our [Gemini vs ChatGPT](/en/posts/gemini-vs-chatgpt-2026) breakdown makes the call easier.

**Search and research.** If you want an engine that answers with citations, Perplexity beats classic blue links, while Google's AI Overviews still reach the widest everyday audience. Our [Perplexity vs Google](/en/posts/perplexity-vs-google-ai-search) comparison shows where each one wins.

**Image generation.** For artistic, stylized visuals, Midjourney remains the reference point; if you want a free and fast option, Google's Nano Banana Pro is a strong contender. We ranked the leaders in our [best AI image generators of 2026](/en/posts/best-ai-image-generators-2026) roundup.

**Office work.** If you live inside Word, Excel, and Teams, Microsoft Copilot is the natural pick; if you use Google Workspace, Gemini does the same job in its own ecosystem. The real value of both comes from arriving inside the office subscription you already pay for.

**Coding.** For code, the trio of ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini leads the pack — Claude on long-file analysis, ChatGPT on breadth, and Gemini on integration with Google tools.

**Content and social media.** If you produce posts, headlines, and short copy regularly, Claude's tone and long context earn their keep here; for a practical workflow, see our guide on [how to use Claude for social media](/en/posts/how-to-use-claude-for-social-media).

I'll be honest: most people do not need to pay for more than one assistant at a time. Either ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro, at $20 a month, covers the vast majority of everyday work — the $100–$300 tiers above them rarely make sense for individuals.

## How fast adoption is moving

The headline is not just who leads but how fast the whole category is growing. ChatGPT became the fastest app in history to cross 1 billion monthly active users, hitting the mark about three years after launch. Google Gemini more than doubled in a year, climbing from 400 million monthly active users to over 900 million between the 2025 and 2026 Google I/O events. Claude's web traffic grew roughly 770% between September 2024 and March 2026.

In other words, the ranking above is a snapshot of a market that still reshuffles every quarter. New tiers, new models, and price cuts land almost monthly — and with free tiers this capable, there is little reason not to keep testing the tools you have not tried yet.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most used AI tool in 2026?

ChatGPT. It reached roughly 1 billion monthly active app users in June 2026 and is the most-visited AI website on Similarweb. Google Gemini and Anthropic's Claude follow in second and third.

### Which AI is better: ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude?

There is no single winner; it depends on the job. ChatGPT leads on all-round use and breadth, Gemini on multimodal tasks and Google integration, and Claude on long-document analysis and careful writing. All three offer capable free tiers, so trying them side by side is the best way to compare.

### What is the best free AI tool right now?

For general use, the free tiers of ChatGPT and Gemini are the strongest options, and both run capable models (ChatGPT's free tier has shown ads since February 2026). For cited search, Perplexity's free tier stands out; for images, Google's Nano Banana Pro.

For chat and writing, ChatGPT or Claude; for cited search, Perplexity; for images, Midjourney or Nano Banana; for office work, Microsoft Copilot or Gemini. For more comparisons and guides, browse our [AI category](/en/category/ai).
